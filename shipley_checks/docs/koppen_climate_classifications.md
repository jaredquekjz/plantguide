# Köppen-Geiger Climate Classification

Source: [Oregon State University - Permaculture Design](https://open.oregonstate.education/permaculturedesign/back-matter/koppen-geiger-classification-descriptions/)

## Main Climate Groups

### A - Tropical (Megathermal)
Average temperature of 18°C or higher every month with significant precipitation

- **Af**: Tropical rainforest - at least 60mm monthly precipitation
- **Am**: Tropical monsoon - driest month under 60mm but over 4% annual total
- **Aw**: Tropical savanna - driest month under 60mm and under 4% annual total
- **As**: Tropical savanna variant - driest month under 60mm and under 4% annual total

### B - Dry (Arid and Semiarid)
Defined by little precipitation; threshold calculated from temperature and seasonal distribution

#### BW - Arid Desert
Below 50% of precipitation threshold
- **BWh**: Hot desert
- **BWk**: Cold desert
- **BWn**: Mild desert

#### BS - Semi-arid Steppe
50-100% of precipitation threshold
- **BSh**: Hot semi-arid
- **BSk**: Cold semi-arid
- **BSn**: Mild semi-arid

### C - Temperate (Mesothermal)
Coldest month averages 0-18°C with at least one month above 10°C

#### Cf - No dry season
- **Cfa**: Humid subtropical - warmest month above 22°C, no dry season
- **Cfb**: Temperate oceanic - all months below 22°C, no dry season
- **Cfc**: Subpolar oceanic - 1-3 months above 10°C, no dry season

#### Cw - Dry winters
- **Cwa**: Monsoon-influenced humid subtropical with dry winters
- **Cwb**: Subtropical highland - dry winters, all months below 22°C
- **Cwc**: Cold subtropical highland - 1-3 months above 10°C, dry winters

#### Cs - Dry summers (Mediterranean)
- **Csa**: Hot-summer Mediterranean with dry summers
- **Csb**: Warm-summer Mediterranean with dry summers
- **Csc**: Cool-summer Mediterranean - 1-3 months above 10°C, dry summers

### D - Continental (Microthermal)
At least one month below 0°C and one month above 10°C

#### Df - No dry season
- **Dfa**: Hot-summer humid continental - no dry season
- **Dfb**: Warm-summer humid continental - no dry season
- **Dfc**: Subarctic - 1-3 months above 10°C, no dry season
- **Dfd**: Extremely cold subarctic (below −38°C) - no dry season

#### Dw - Dry winters
- **Dwa**: Monsoon-influenced hot-summer continental
- **Dwb**: Monsoon-influenced warm-summer continental
- **Dwc**: Monsoon-influenced subarctic
- **Dwd**: Monsoon-influenced extremely cold subarctic

#### Ds - Dry summers
- **Dsa**: Hot, dry-summer continental
- **Dsb**: Warm, dry-summer continental
- **Dsc**: Dry-summer subarctic
- **Dsd**: Extremely cold, dry-summer subarctic

### E - Polar and Alpine (Montane)
Every month averages below 10°C

- **ET**: Tundra - all months 0-10°C (mild tundra)
- **ETf**: Cold tundra - at least one month below 0°C
- **EF**: Ice cap climate - all months below 0°C

## Layman-Friendly Descriptions (for Encyclopedia)

These simplified descriptions are used in the plant encyclopedia to help gardeners understand climate zones:

### Tropical (A)
- **Af**: "Tropical rainforest - warm & wet year-round"
- **Am**: "Tropical monsoon - warm year-round with seasonal heavy rains"
- **Aw/As**: "Tropical savanna - warm year-round with distinct wet/dry seasons"

### Arid (B)
- **BWh**: "Hot desert - very hot & dry year-round"
- **BWk**: "Cold desert - dry with cold winters"
- **BSh**: "Hot semi-arid - hot with limited rainfall"
- **BSk**: "Cold semi-arid - cool to cold with limited rainfall"

### Temperate (C)
- **Cfa**: "Humid subtropical - hot humid summers, mild winters, rain year-round"
- **Cfb**: "Temperate oceanic - mild year-round, no dry season (typical Western Europe)"
- **Cfc**: "Subpolar oceanic - cool summers, mild winters, wet year-round"
- **Csa**: "Hot-summer Mediterranean - hot dry summers, mild wet winters"
- **Csb**: "Warm-summer Mediterranean - warm dry summers, mild wet winters"
- **Csc**: "Cool-summer Mediterranean - cool summers, mild wet winters"
- **Cwa**: "Humid subtropical - hot summers, dry winters"
- **Cwb/Cwc**: "Subtropical highland - mild with dry winters"

### Continental (D)
- **Dfa/Dfb**: "Humid continental - warm summers, cold snowy winters"
- **Dfc/Dfd**: "Subarctic - short cool summers, very cold winters"
- **Dwa/Dwb/Dwc/Dwd**: "Monsoon continental - dry cold winters, wet summers"
- **Dsa/Dsb/Dsc/Dsd**: "Continental Mediterranean - dry summers, cold winters"

### Polar (E)
- **ET**: "Tundra - very cold, short growing season"
- **EF**: "Ice cap - frozen year-round"

## Usage in Encyclopedia

The Köppen zone provides a quick overview of the overall climate type where plant populations are most commonly found. It combines temperature, rainfall, and seasonality patterns into a single classification.

For European plants, the most common zones are:
- **Cfb**: Temperate oceanic (Western Europe - UK, Ireland, Netherlands, Belgium)
- **Cfa**: Humid subtropical (Southeast Europe)
- **Csb**: Warm-summer Mediterranean (Mediterranean coast)
- **Dfb**: Humid continental (Central/Eastern Europe)
- **Dfc**: Subarctic (Northern Scandinavia)
- **ET**: Tundra (Arctic regions, high mountains)
